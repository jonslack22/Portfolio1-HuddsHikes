Welcome to the homepage of Hudds Hikers!

We are a new, independent rambling group operating from the heart of Kirklees, Huddersfield. Founded in 2020 by Jonathan Slack, Kevin Smith and Steven Moss, our aim is to provide accessible rambling experiences to as wide an audience as possible, including those with minor physical and mental disabilities. Our schedule includes:

  • 5-6 mile walks on Tuesday and Thursday evenings during summer
  • "B" grade walks of 6-9 miles on Sundays
  • In summer, monthly slower-paced "A-minus" walks for those who would like a longer walk of up to 11 miles, typically on Saturdays
  • Easy monthly Saturday walks of up to 5 miles on Saturdays and Sundays (C-grade walks) for those looking to build up fitness or become more active, and new members of the group.

Please note: as a group aiming to cater towards disabilities, we don't currently offer walks of grade "A" or higher. This may be subject to change as the fitness levels and capabilities of the group memebers change, however.

By default, all our walks are circular.

Why Ramble with Us?

Become part of an inclusive group that supports rambling access to those with minor physical disabilities and learning disabilities. At Hudds Hikes, we believe that the countryside should be as accessible as possible.

Our head walkers are all first-aid trained and equipped, and knowledgeable of emergency procedures should your safety be in doubt.

With an extensive knowledge of the landscapes and history comprising our walks in the Kirklees and Pennines districts, you won't receive a better countryside tour from anyone else!

We offer an extensive social scene throughout the calendar year, including meals out, holidays and walks further afield. We're quite partial to a pint and a pub-cooked meal after our walks, as well!
